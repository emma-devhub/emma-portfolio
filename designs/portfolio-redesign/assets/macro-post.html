Hammack Signals Need for an Open-Minded Policy Approach

Cleveland Fed President Hammack signaled that policymakers must remain open-minded on the future path of interest rates, citing significant uncertainty in the current economic environment.

Because monetary policy operates with long and variable lags, she emphasized, the committee must look ahead to the next six to twelve months when judging the appropriate stance to achieve its dual mandate.

"When I hear uncertainty about the economy, that translates into uncertainty around policy, which means we need to be open-minded about where it may need to go."

Hammack noted risks from an entrenched inflationary mindset, pointing to the persistent failure to meet the 2% objective over the past five years.

While acknowledging that global shocks have moved prices, she expressed concern that the cumulative burden of inflation is now landing squarely on American households, and that it is difficult to look through a miss running five years long.

Markets read the remarks as a reminder that the bar to cut may sit higher than the dots imply, even as the front end keeps repricing.
